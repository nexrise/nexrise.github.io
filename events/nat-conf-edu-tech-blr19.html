Introduction

WHAT IS THE NCET 2019?

With the advancement in applied sciences and technology, these fields have become increasingly sophisticated and interdisciplinary related. In line with this trend, the National Conference on Educational Technology (NCET 2019) is expected to provide an international platform that allows researchers, academicians and industry experts to share and discuss their latest research, ideas, and survey reports in the theoretical and practical areas regarding all aspects of Technology and Applied Sciences. Prospective authors are welcome to submit their full papers or abstracts. All submissions to the conference will go through blind review processes by at least two independent peers.

NCET 2019 enables collaboration between science and technologists in the academic and industrial fields as well as international networks. The conference allows for substantial opportunities for presentation and academic discourse. In addition, poster sessions and exhibitions offer ample opportunity for information exchange among delegates and participants on a more personal level, especially for those who are looking for collaborative opportunities.

NCET 2019 - THE BIG IDEA

Intelligent machines have continued to make an ever-greater impact on the apps, services and touch points we come into contact with every day. This “revolution” of artificial intelligence is poised to change everything, and will empower companies to deliver better, smarter services with greater ease and on a broader scale than ever before.

We are already using voice-recognition systems, trailing driverless cars and utilizing robots to enhance customer services. The advent of certain technologies - inexpensive high-speed internet, secure cloud storage, mobility solutions and low cost devices - has allowed the imagined possibilities of the past to become reality.

AI is already having an impact on most industries - not only is it re-shaping the technology that companies use, but also changing how companies organize and operate their businesses. Because of AI we are beginning to see seismic advances in health, transport, banking, energy, education and many other domains. On many estimates, over the next 10 years enterprise spending on AI technology will increase from $200m to $50billion.

Clearly, the AI revolution is here - but we’ve only scratched the surface on what’s to come. AI will increasingly transform the way we live and work. It’s the businesses who embrace this transformation who will reap the biggest rewards as failure to adapt is far more detrimental than early adoption.
